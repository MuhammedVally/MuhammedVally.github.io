Thesis Project

Impact of AC-Side Filters on Grid-Forming Converters

Investigated how different AC-side filter topologies (L, LC, LCL) affect the dynamic response of grid-forming (GFM) converters. The study analyzed voltage and frequency step responses, with a focus on rise time, settling time, and overshoot. Simulations were conducted in MATLAB/Simulink and validated using Typhoon HIL real-time hardware-in-the-loop (CHIL) testing.

MATLAB/Simulink Typhoon HIL GFM Converters AC-Side Filters
Robotics & Control

Micromouse Maze-Solving Robot

Developed an autonomous robot capable of navigating and solving a 7x7 maze using infrared sensors, a flood-fill algorithm, and precise motion control. Implemented mapping, pathfinding, and position tracking with real-time decision-making on an STM32 microcontroller. Performance was enhanced through PID-based line following, IMU-assisted turning, and wall-sensing optimizations. Hardware improvements included shielding sensors from ambient light and adding additional downward sensors for reliable position tracking.

STM32 PID Control IMU Flood-Fill Algorithm
